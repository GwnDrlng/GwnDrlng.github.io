Digitizing Group Retirement Enrolment at Scale

Canada's first fully digital, mobile-responsive Group Retirement enrolment platform. Five dispersed delivery teams, SAFe enterprise delivery, AODA compliance, ATO prevention, and behavioural economics applied to the most important financial decision most plan members will make.

Company Manulife Financial
Role Product Manager, Digital Enrolment
Timeline Oct 2015 – Mar 2021
Domain Fintech · Group Retirement · Enterprise SaaS

The context

In 2015, Manulife's Group Retirement enrolment was largely paper-based or ran through systems that hadn't been designed for how plan members actually lived and worked. A new employee joining a company retirement plan needed to navigate materials, fill out forms, and make investment decisions — often with no guidance, no mobile support, and no clear path from "I received the plan package" to "I'm enrolled and invested."

The business case was clear: a digital, mobile-responsive enrolment experience would reduce administrative friction for plan administrators, reduce call centre load for Manulife, and — most importantly — improve enrolment rates and retirement contribution outcomes for members.

The complexity

This wasn't a startup building an MVP. It was an enterprise financial services transformation under real constraints:

  • Regulatory: AODA compliance was non-negotiable — every surface had to meet accessibility standards. Canadian financial services data governance governed what we could store, transmit, and display.
  • Security: Account takeover prevention was a first-class requirement. Plan members' retirement assets were on the other side of that login screen.
  • Organizational: Five dispersed delivery teams — front-end UI through back-end data governance — in a SAFe Agile Enterprise environment. Alignment across that many teams, with differing sprint rhythms and backlogs, required constant negotiation and sequencing discipline.
  • Stakeholder: Sales, Operations, Investment Product, Marketing, plan Advisors, plan Consultants, and Executives all had skin in the outcome and different definitions of success.

What I built

Canada's first digital Group Retirement enrolment platform

The enrolment platform launched as Manulife's first fully web-based, mobile-responsive Group Retirement savings enrolment experience for plan members. From any device, a new member could enrol, select investment options, and complete contribution setup without a paper form or a phone call.

Manulife's Group Retirement plan members can now enrol, make investment choices, and set up contributions entirely through a responsive web experience — from any device, at any time.

Dual-vendor AODA investment fund site

A significant workstream was leading the creation of a dual-vendor AODA-compliant investment fund site — the reference point plan members and advisors use to review fund performance, descriptions, and positioning. The dual-vendor approach reduced costs by $2M+ annually while meeting accessibility requirements that the previous implementation didn't satisfy.

ATO prevention and IAM

Account takeover prevention was shaped at the product level — defining how Identity and Access Management standards would integrate with the web enrolment experience, across all Manulife business segments. In group retirement, IAM isn't just a security feature: it's the gate between a member's identity and their retirement savings. Getting that architecture right mattered.

Behavioural economics in the enrolment UX

The most interesting design work was applying behavioural economics principles to the contribution and investment selection flows. Plan members face a set of decisions during enrolment — how much to contribute, which funds to allocate to — that most of them aren't equipped to make with confidence. The default choices, the framing of options, the order of decisions, and the use of comparisons all influence outcomes significantly.

I applied persona-driven UX hypothesis testing — building hypotheses about which nudges, defaults, and framing approaches would improve enrolment completion rates, contribution levels, and investment diversification. Results informed both the product UX and future engagement campaigns.

What I learned

Enterprise-scale product work at Manulife taught me two things I've carried to every role since.

First: SAFe at scale is a stakeholder management problem masquerading as an Agile problem. Five teams can share a program increment and still be working toward different goals if the product vision isn't communicated clearly and repeatedly. The most valuable thing I did in those 5+ years wasn't writing the best user stories — it was making sure every team understood why their piece mattered and how it connected to what the plan member would experience.

Second: financial product UX is never just UX. The decisions a plan member makes during enrolment — contribution rate, fund allocation, beneficiary designation — have 30-year consequences. Taking that seriously means applying real research, real hypothesis testing, and real iteration to the moments that matter most. Behavioural economics gave us a framework for doing that rigorously instead of just intuitively.
