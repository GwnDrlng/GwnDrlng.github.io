Digitizing Group Retirement Enrolment at Scale

Canada's first fully digital, mobile-responsive Group Retirement enrolment platform. Five dispersed delivery teams, SAFe enterprise delivery, AODA compliance, ATO prevention, and behavioural economics applied to the most important financial decision most plan members will make.

Company Manulife Financial
Role Product Manager, Digital Enrolment
Timeline Oct 2015 – Mar 2021
Domain Fintech · Group Retirement · Enterprise SaaS

The context

Paper enrolment was confusing, and the help mostly ended after one meeting with the plan's advisor. A new employee joining a company retirement plan had to navigate materials, fill out forms, and make investment decisions with no guidance, no mobile support, and no clear path from "I received the plan package" to "I'm enrolled and invested." Many never contributed at all.

The business case was clear. A digital, mobile-responsive enrolment experience would reduce administrative friction for plan administrators, reduce call centre load for Manulife, and, most importantly, improve enrolment rates and retirement contribution outcomes for members.

The complexity

This was an enterprise financial services transformation under real constraints, a long way from a startup MVP:

  • Regulatory: AODA compliance was non-negotiable. Every surface had to meet accessibility standards, and Canadian financial services data governance governed what we could store, transmit, and display.
  • Security: Account takeover prevention was a first-class requirement. Plan members' retirement assets sat on the other side of that login screen.
  • Organizational: Five dispersed delivery teams spanned front-end UI through back-end data governance in a SAFe Agile enterprise environment. Alignment across that many teams, each with its own sprint rhythm and backlog, required constant negotiation and sequencing discipline.
  • Stakeholder: Sales, Operations, Investment Product, Marketing, plan Advisors, plan Consultants, and Executives all had skin in the outcome and different definitions of success.

What the work looked like

Personas that made decisions

We built personas from our own research combined with Manulife's Canadian research, and they earned their keep as a decision tool. When a design or prioritization question came up, the team could recall exactly who the feature was for and why a choice should go in that member's favour, every time. The personas kept a five-team program pointed at the same human being.

Canada's first digital Group Retirement enrolment platform

The platform launched as Manulife's first fully web-based, mobile-responsive Group Retirement enrolment experience. From any device, a new member could enrol, select investment options, and complete contribution setup without a paper form or a phone call.

Manulife's Group Retirement plan members can now enrol, make investment choices, and set up contributions entirely through a responsive web experience, from any device, at any time.

Dual-vendor AODA investment fund site

A significant workstream was leading the creation of a dual-vendor, AODA-compliant investment fund site, the reference point plan members and advisors use to review fund performance, descriptions, and positioning. The dual-vendor approach reduced costs by $2M+ annually while meeting accessibility requirements the previous implementation did not satisfy.

ATO prevention and IAM

Account takeover prevention was shaped at the product level, defining how Identity and Access Management standards would integrate with the web enrolment experience across all Manulife business segments. In group retirement, IAM is the gate between a member's identity and their retirement savings. Getting that architecture right mattered.

Behavioural economics in the enrolment UX

The most interesting design work applied behavioural economics to the contribution and investment selection flows. Plan members face decisions during enrolment, how much to contribute and which funds to allocate to, that most are not equipped to make with confidence. Default choices, option framing, decision order, and comparisons all influence outcomes significantly.

Persona-driven UX hypothesis testing gave the work rigour. We built hypotheses about which nudges, defaults, and framing approaches would improve enrolment completion, contribution levels, and investment diversification, then let results inform both the product UX and future engagement campaigns.

What I learned

Enterprise-scale product work at Manulife taught me two things I have carried to every role since.

First: SAFe at scale is a stakeholder management problem masquerading as an Agile problem. Five teams can share a program increment and still work toward different goals if the product vision is not communicated clearly and repeatedly. The most valuable thing I did in those five-plus years was making sure every team understood why their piece mattered and how it connected to what the plan member would experience.

Second: financial product UX is never just UX. The decisions a plan member makes during enrolment, contribution rate, fund allocation, beneficiary designation, carry 30-year consequences. Taking that seriously means applying real research, real hypothesis testing, and real iteration to the moments that matter most. Behavioural economics gave us a framework for doing that rigorously instead of intuitively.
