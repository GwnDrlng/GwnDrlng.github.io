Gwen Darling

Director-level Product Leader. 10+ years in B2B SaaS across cybersecurity, digital forensics, and fintech. P&L owner. Fast decision-maker. Deeply collaborative.

How I got here

I didn't start in product — I started at a 75-person tea company where I reported directly to the CEO and figured out that the most leverage I had was at the intersection of customer understanding and operational systems. That role taught me that the best product decisions aren't made in isolation: they're made by people who've been close enough to the problem to know what matters and fast enough to make the call before the window closes.

From Steeped Tea I went to Manulife, where I spent five years learning what product work looks like at enterprise scale: five dispersed delivery teams, regulatory constraints, SAFe, and stakeholders from front-line advisors to C-suite. Then Magnet Forensics, where the customers were law enforcement agencies using our tools to investigate serious crimes. Then Arctic Wolf, where I built a product line from zero that the industry validated within two years.

The domains have been different. The underlying work has been consistent: understand the problem before designing the solution, make the call clearly, move fast, and stay close to the customer and the data in roughly equal measure.

How I work

I make decisions fast — and I've learned that's a skill, not just a personality trait. Fast decisions aren't reckless ones; they're decisions made with enough information to be directionally correct, combined with the willingness to update when new data arrives. Waiting for certainty is usually just a way to avoid making a call.

I work best when I'm collaborating with strong teams, not just coordinating with them. The work is better when engineers, designers, and GTM partners are part of the problem-framing, not just recipients of a requirements document.

I'm drawn to high-stakes domains — not for the prestige, but because the feedback is clearer. When a forensic investigator uses your triage tool in the field, or when a company activates an IR retainer after a breach, you know fast whether the product held up. That clarity makes the work better.

What I'm looking for

I'm seeking a Director, Head, Lead, or Staff Product Management role with genuine ownership — roadmap, strategy, and outcomes, not just execution. I want to work on a product that matters, with a team that moves, in a company that's honest about where it is and where it's going.

Domain-wise: B2B SaaS, cybersecurity, fintech, AI — all serious fits. I'm also genuinely open to sectors I haven't been in yet if the problem is interesting and the people are excellent.

Remote is preferred. Hybrid in the GTA or Ottawa region is workable. I'm able to work across EST/CST overlap and have navigated international market expansion from a Canadian base.

What I am not looking for

A task-management role masquerading as a product role. A company where "stakeholder alignment" means getting approval from seventeen people before making any decision. A role where the product strategy is owned elsewhere and I'm executing someone else's roadmap.
